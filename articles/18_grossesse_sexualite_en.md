# Pregnancy and Sexuality: Evolution and Advice

Sexuality during pregnancy is a subject that raises many questions and sometimes concerns for future parents. With bodily changes, hormonal fluctuations, and concerns about the baby's well-being, it is normal for the couple's intimate life to evolve during this period. This article frankly addresses the different aspects of sexuality during pregnancy, trimester by trimester, and offers practical advice for maintaining a fulfilling intimate life.

## Common Misconceptions About Sex During Pregnancy

Before exploring the specifics of each trimester, let's start by debunking some persistent myths:

### "Sexual Intercourse Can Harm the Baby"

**Reality**: In a normal pregnancy without complications, sexual intercourse presents no danger to the baby. The baby is perfectly protected by:
- The amniotic sac
- The mucus plug sealing the cervix
- The uterine muscles

The penis, regardless of its size, never comes into contact with the baby.

### "Orgasm Can Cause Premature Labor"

**Reality**: Uterine contractions related to orgasm are different from labor contractions. In a pregnancy without complications, they do not trigger labor. However, at the end of pregnancy, when the body is already ready, orgasm can sometimes contribute to triggering labor, which is not necessarily problematic.

### "Desire Necessarily Disappears During Pregnancy"

**Reality**: Sexual desire during pregnancy varies considerably from one woman to another and can fluctuate throughout the trimesters. Some women experience increased libido, especially in the second trimester, while others may feel a temporary decrease in desire.

### "You Must Abstain at the End of Pregnancy"

**Reality**: In the absence of specific medical contraindications, sexual intercourse is allowed until the end of pregnancy. It can even favor the natural onset of labor when the body is ready, thanks to prostaglandins contained in sperm and mild contractions caused by orgasm.

## Physiological Changes Influencing Sexuality

Pregnancy causes numerous physiological changes that can affect sexuality:

### Hormonal Changes

- **Increase in estrogen and progesterone**: Can lead to breast hypersensitivity, increased vaginal lubrication, and sometimes increased libido.
- **Increased blood flow** in the pelvic region: Can intensify sensations during intercourse and facilitate orgasm.
- **Hormonal fluctuations**: Can cause mood swings and affect desire.

### Bodily Changes

- **Silhouette changes**: Can affect body image and self-confidence.
- **Increased breast volume**: Often accompanied by hypersensitivity.
- **Rounding belly**: Requires adapting sexual positions.
- **Vaginal congestion**: Can increase sensitivity and pleasure, but also create discomfort.

### Emotional Changes

- **Concerns about the baby**: Can create distractions during intimacy.
- **Transition to parenthood**: Can change self-perception and perception of the partner.
- **Fatigue and stress**: Can decrease availability for intimacy.
- **Feeling of increased connection** with the partner: Can strengthen emotional intimacy.

## Sexuality Trimester by Trimester

### First Trimester (Weeks 1 to 13)

The first trimester is often marked by symptoms that can affect sex life:

#### Potential Challenges
- **Nausea and fatigue**: These common symptoms can significantly reduce libido.
- **Breast hypersensitivity**: Can make usual caresses uncomfortable.
- **Fears about miscarriage**: Although sexual intercourse does not cause miscarriage in a normal pregnancy, this worry is frequent.
- **Emotional adaptation** to pregnancy: Time to assimilate the news can temporarily affect desire.

#### Advice for This Trimester
- **Prioritize times when fatigue and nausea are less present**, often late in the day.
- **Communicate about areas that have become sensitive** or uncomfortable.
- **Explore other forms of intimacy** if full intercourse is not desired: cuddling, massages, caresses.
- **Reassure each other** about the changes happening in your relationship.

### Second Trimester (Weeks 14 to 27)

Often considered the "honeymoon" of pregnancy, the second trimester is generally more favorable for sexual fulfillment:

#### Advantages of This Period
- **Decrease in nausea and fatigue** for most women.
- **Increased blood flow** in the pelvic region, potentially intensifying pleasure.
- **Belly still moderately developed**, allowing a variety of positions.
- **Feeling of well-being** related to accepting the pregnancy and decreasing worries.

#### Particularities to Note
- **Possible increase in vaginal secretions**: Completely normal and even beneficial for intercourse.
- **New sensations**: Some women discover increased sensitivity and more intense orgasms.
- **First baby movements**: Can be felt during intercourse, without danger to the baby.

#### Advice for This Trimester
- **Take advantage of this generally favorable period** to reconnect with intimacy if the first trimester was difficult.
- **Explore new positions** if your habits become less comfortable.
- **Reassure your partner** who may fear "disturbing" the baby or hurting you.
- **Hydrate well** to compensate for increased vaginal secretions.

### Third Trimester (Weeks 28 to 40)

The last trimester brings its share of challenges for intimate life, but with adaptation, sexuality can remain fulfilling:

#### Challenges of This Period
- **Significant abdominal volume**: Limits certain positions and can create discomfort.
- **Faster shortness of breath**: Due to pressure on the diaphragm.
- **Braxton Hicks contractions**: Can be triggered by sexual arousal.
- **Anxiety related to approaching childbirth**: Can decrease mental availability.
- **General discomfort**: Heavy legs, lower back pain, reflux...

#### Recommended Adaptations
- **Prioritize positions that do not put pressure on the abdomen**:
  * Woman on top, controlling depth and pace
  * Spoons (side-lying)
  * Woman sitting on partner's lap
  * Partner behind, woman on all fours or leaning forward
- **Slow down the pace** and plan breaks if necessary.
- **Use pillows** to support the belly and back.
- **Explore foreplay more** and caresses if full intercourse becomes uncomfortable.

## Medical Contraindications

In certain situations, your doctor or midwife may recommend limiting or temporarily avoiding sexual intercourse:

### Absolute Contraindications
- **Premature rupture of membranes** (water breaking)
- **Placenta previa** (placenta covering the cervix)
- **Unexplained vaginal bleeding**
- **Threat of premature labor**
- **Active genital infections** in either partner

### Situations Requiring Precautions
- **Multiple pregnancy**: Increased caution, especially in the third trimester.
- **History of miscarriage** or premature birth: Your doctor may recommend restrictions.
- **Incompetent cervix**: May adhere temporary abstinence.
- **Certain pregnancy pathologies** like preeclampsia.

In case of doubt, do not hesitate to consult your healthcare professional who can give you advice adapted to your specific situation.

## Beyond Penetration: Diversifying Intimacy

Sexuality is not limited to intercourse with penetration. Pregnancy is an opportunity to explore other forms of intimacy:

### Alternatives to Vaginal Penetration
- **Manual stimulation**: Can be particularly pleasant due to increased blood flow.
- **Oral stimulation**: Remains a safe option throughout pregnancy (in the absence of infection).
- **Sensual massages**: Particularly welcome to relieve pregnancy muscle tension.
- **Use of sex toys**: Possible with caution and good hygiene.
- **Emotional intimacy**: Moments of tenderness, complicity, and communication.

### Specific Precautions
- **Avoid blowing air into the vagina** during oral sex (theoretical risk of embolism).
- **Be attentive to hygiene** of hands and sex toys to avoid infections.
- **Communicate clearly** about what is comfortable or not.

## Psychological and Emotional Dimension

Sexuality during pregnancy involves an important psychological dimension that deserves attention:

### For the Future Mother
- **Evolving body image**: Acceptance of bodily changes strongly influences desire and pleasure.
- **Double identity**: Reconciling her role as a future mother with that of a woman and sexual partner.
- **Concerns** about childbirth, motherhood, upcoming life changes.
- **Increased need for tenderness and reassurance**.

### For the Partner
- **Fears of hurting** the baby or the future mother.
- **Adapting to the partner's new silhouette**.
- **Projection into the role of parent**.
- **Possible feeling of exclusion** facing the developing mother-child privileged relationship.

### For the Couple
- **Opportunity to strengthen communication** about each other's needs and limits.
- **Preparation for transition to parenthood**.
- **Redefining intimacy** beyond the purely sexual aspect.
- **Learning adaptation** to changes, an essential skill for future life as parents.

## Practical Advice for a Fulfilling Sexuality During Pregnancy

### Communication and Consent
- **Clearly express your sensations**: What was pleasant before pregnancy may no longer be, and vice versa.
- **Mutually respect your limits**: Desire can fluctuate considerably during this period.
- **Share your worries** rather than letting them create distance.

### Comfort and Well-being
- **Empty your bladder before intercourse** to reduce pressure.
- **Use a water-based lubricant** if necessary, despite the natural increase in secretions.
- **Plan pillows and cushions** to support the body and find comfortable positions.
- **Take your time**: Foreplay is essential for relaxation and pleasure.

## Sexuality After Childbirth: Anticipating the Transition

Although this article focuses on pregnancy, it is useful to briefly mention the postpartum period to better prepare for it:

### Resuming Sexual Intercourse
- **Medical recommendation**: Generally 4 to 6 weeks after delivery, after the postnatal visit.
- **Gradual resumption**: The first time can be uncomfortable, take your time.
- **Lubrication**: Often necessary due to hormonal changes, especially if breastfeeding.

## Conclusion

Sexuality during pregnancy is a unique experience that evolves over the trimesters. Far from being a period of mandatory abstinence, it can be experienced as a time of exploration, adaptation, and deepening of the couple's intimacy.

The keys to fulfilling sexuality during this period are open communication, adaptation to bodily and emotional changes, and creativity to find new ways to share intimacy.

Remember that every pregnancy is unique, just like every couple. There is no absolute norm regarding desire and sexual practices during pregnancy - the important thing is to find what suits you both, respecting everyone's well-being and the safety of the pregnancy.
