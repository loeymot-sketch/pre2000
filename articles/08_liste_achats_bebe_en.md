# Preparing for Baby's Arrival: Essential Shopping List

The arrival of a baby is a wonderful event that requires careful preparation. Between excitement and apprehension, it can be difficult to know exactly what you will need to welcome your newborn. This article offers you a complete list of essential purchases to plan, organized by categories, to help you calmly prepare for your baby's arrival.

## When to Start Shopping?

The question of timing is important. Ideally, start your main purchases after the second-trimester morphological ultrasound (around 22 weeks), when you are reassured about the smooth progress of the pregnancy. However, you can spread your purchases over several months to distribute the expenses.

Plan to have the essentials completed about one month before the expected due date, as babies can arrive earlier than expected. However, keep the receipts and don't remove all the tags before birth, in case you need to make exchanges.

## Sleeping

Sleep takes up a large part of a newborn's life. Here is what you will need to ensure their comfort and safety during their rest hours:

### The Crib or Bed

- **Slatted Crib**: If you opt directly for a slatted crib, choose one meeting European safety standards (EN 716), with a spacing between slats between 4.5 and 6.5 cm.
- **Bassinet or Moses Basket**: For the first months, a bassinet or Moses basket may be more suitable and easier to place in your room.
- **Co-Sleeper**: Co-sleeping bassinets, which attach to the parental bed, are a popular option for facilitating nighttime breastfeeding and reassuring new parents.

### Bedding

- **Mattress**: Firm, in the exact dimensions of the crib or bassinet, to avoid any suffocation risk.
- **Waterproof Mattress Protectors**: Plan at least two so you can change them in case of accidents.
- **Fitted Sheets**: Minimum 3 to 4, adapted to the mattress dimensions.
- **Sleeping Bags or Sleep Sacks**: 2 to 3 in newborn size, adapted to the season (light for summer, warm for winter).

Avoid pillows, duvets, blankets, and crib bumpers that present suffocation risks for newborns.

### Sleep Accessories

- **Mobile**: Not essential but can help visually stimulate baby and soothe them.
- **Night Light**: Useful for nighttime feedings without disturbing baby's sleep.
- **Baby Monitor**: Audio or video baby monitor to watch baby from a distance.
- **Room Thermometer**: To maintain an ideal temperature in the room (18-20°C).

## Hygiene and Care

Daily care takes an important place in a newborn's routine. Here is what you will need:

### The Changing Table

- **Changing Surface**: On a dresser or a standalone changing table with storage.
- **Changing Mat**: With waterproof and easy-to-clean covering.
- **Changing Mat Covers**: 2 to 3 washable covers for more comfort.

### Toiletry Products

- **Baby Bathtub**: With stand or bathtub adaptable to standard bathtub.
- **Bath Seat**: To keep baby safe during bath.
- **Bath Thermometer**: To check that water is at the right temperature (37°C).
- **Gentle Soap or Baby Wash**: Fragrance-free and hypoallergenic.
- **Gentle Shampoo**: Specific for newborns.
- **Liniment**: For cleaning baby's bottom during diaper changes.
- **Saline Solution**: In individual doses for nose and eye cleaning.
- **Diaper Cream**: To prevent diaper rash.
- **Moisturizing Cream**: Adapted to babies' sensitive skin.

### Toiletry Accessories

- **Washcloths**: 6 to 8, baby-specific.
- **Hooded Bath Towels**: 2 to 3, soft and absorbent.
- **Soft Hairbrush**: With soft bristles, special for newborns.
- **Nail Scissors or File**: With rounded tips for safety.
- **Nasal Aspirator**: Manual or electric to clear the nostrils.
- **Medical Thermometer**: Preferably rectal for newborns (more accurate).

### Diaper Changes

- **Diapers**: About 8 to 10 per day for a newborn, approximately 70 diapers per week. Start with a small pack of size 1 (2-5 kg) and a pack of size 2 (3-6 kg), as babies grow fast.
- **Wipes**: Prefer fragrance-free wipes or, better yet, washable cotton squares with water.
- **Diaper Pail**: Ideally with an anti-odor system.
- **Diaper Bag**: With built-in changing mat for outings.

## Feeding

Whether you choose breastfeeding or bottle feeding, some equipment will be necessary:

### For Breastfeeding

- **Nursing Pillow**: For better comfort during feedings.
- **Nursing Bras**: 3 to 4, adapted to your new shape.
- **Nursing Pads**: Disposable or washable to absorb milk leaks.
- **Nipple Cream**: Based on purified lanolin to prevent cracking.
- **Breast Pump**: Manual or electric if you plan to pump your milk.
- **Breast Milk Storage Bags**: If you use a breast pump.

### For Bottle Feeding

- **Bottles**: 6 to 8 bottles of 240 ml (some babies have preferences for certain nipples, don't buy everything in large quantities before testing).
- **Nipples**: Several flow rates (slow for newborns, then medium).
- **Bottle Brush**: For efficiently cleaning bottles.
- **Sterilizer**: Steam, microwave, or cold (not essential but practical for the first months).
- **Bottle Warmer**: Useful but not essential, a bowl of warm water can suffice.
- **Infant Formula**: If you are not breastfeeding, consult your pediatrician for the choice of formula.

### For Later

- **Bibs**: To plan for food diversification (around 4-6 months).
- **Adapted Tableware**: Plates, cutlery, and cups for baby (from 6 months).
- **High Chair**: Necessary from the start of food diversification.

## Clothing

Baby clothes should be comfortable, practical, and adapted to the season. Here are the recommended quantities for newborn size (0-1 month) and 1 month (50-56 cm):

### The Basics

- **Short-Sleeve Bodysuits**: 6 to 8 (essential in every season).
- **Long-Sleeve Bodysuits**: 6 to 8 (for winter or cool evenings).
- **Pajamas/Sleepsuits**: 6 to 8, with front or crotch opening to facilitate diaper changes.
- **Newborn Cardigans**: 3 to 4, in cotton (for the very first days).
- **Socks or Booties**: 4 to 6 pairs.
- **Hats**: 2 (even in summer for the first days).

### According to the Season

#### For Winter
- **Snowsuit**: 1 or 2, warm and enveloping.
- **Vests or Cardigans**: 2 to 3, easy to put on.
- **Swaddling Blanket**: For outings.

#### For Summer
- **Light Rompers**: 3 to 4.
- **Wide-Brimmed Hat**: To protect from the sun.
- **Light Outfit**: 1 or 2 for outings.

### Clothing Tips

- Prioritize cotton clothes, soft and breathable.
- Choose easy-to-put-on clothes, with snaps or zippers (avoid numerous buttons).
- Wash all new clothes before first use with a baby-specific detergent.
- Don't buy too many newborn-size clothes, as some babies outgrow this size very quickly.

## Transportation

To transport baby safely, several items of equipment are necessary:

### In the Car

- **Car Seat**: Mandatory from the hospital discharge, choose an approved model for newborns (group 0+ or i-Size). Some can clip onto the stroller to form a "travel system."

### For Walks

- **Stroller**: Ideally with a full recline position for newborns, or compatible with the car seat.
- **Rain Cover**: To protect baby from bad weather.
- **Umbrella or Parasol**: For sunny walks.
- **Diaper Bag**: With everything needed for outings (diapers, bottles, etc.).

### For Carrying Baby

- **Baby Wrap or Ergonomic Baby Carrier**: Babywearing promotes the attachment bond and can soothe babies who need contact.

## Comfort and Development

These accessories are not indispensable but can improve baby's comfort and promote their development:

### To Soothe Baby

- **Pacifiers**: 2 to 4 if you choose to use them (some babies refuse them).
- **Comfort Toy or Stuffed Animal**: For comfort (from 3 months for sleep).
- **Bouncer**: To comfortably seat baby during the day.
- **Play Mat**: For tummy time play moments.
- **Rattles and Development Toys**: A few are enough for the first months.
- **Musical Mobile**: To stimulate baby's sight and hearing.

## Baby's Medicine Cabinet

Prepare a small medicine cabinet with the essentials:

- **Saline Solution**: In individual doses.
- **Gentle Antiseptic**: For minor wounds.
- **Soothing Cream**: For skin irritations.
- **Medical Thermometer**: Preferably rectal for newborns.
- **Nasal Aspirator**: To clear the nasal passages.
- **Pediatric Paracetamol Suspension**: To be used only on medical advice.
- **Sterile Gauze and Bandages**: For minor care.

## Tips for Optimizing Your Budget

The arrival of a baby represents a significant financial investment. Here are some tips to best manage your budget:

### Distinguish Essential from Superfluous

Focus first on indispensable items (safe sleeping area, car seat, basic hygiene products) before buying less essential accessories.

### Prefer Second-Hand for Certain Items

Many items can be purchased second-hand in excellent condition:
- Clothes (which are worn for a short time)
- Stroller
- Bouncer
- Development toys

However, certain items should always be bought new for safety reasons:
- Bed mattress
- Car seat (unless you know its history perfectly)
- Nipples and bottles

### Ask Your Circle

Family and friends are often happy to help with baby's equipment. Do not hesitate to create a birth registry that will help avoid duplicates and receive truly useful gifts.

### Spread Out Purchases

Distribute your purchases over several months to lighten the financial burden. Start with the essentials and gradually supplement.

## Conclusion

Preparing for baby's arrival requires organization, but it is not necessary to have everything on the first day. Focus on the essentials: a safe place to sleep, comfortable clothes, what is needed for feeding and hygiene, as well as an approved car seat.

Remember that each family's needs are different, and that some accessories that seem indispensable to some parents may prove useless to others. Listen to advice, but trust your intuition to determine what will best suit your situation.

Finally, beyond the material aspects, the most important preparation remains that of the heart: love, patience, and kindness are the most precious gifts you will offer your child.
