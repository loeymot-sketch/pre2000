# Rights and Benefits for Expectant and New Mothers

Maternity is a period of great change accompanied by many practical and administrative questions. In France, a comprehensive system of aid and rights exists to support expectant and new mothers. This article presents all the available schemes, from steps to take during pregnancy to aid available after your child's birth.

## Essential Steps During Pregnancy

### Declaration of Pregnancy

The declaration of pregnancy is the first administrative step:

- **When?** Before the end of the 3rd month of pregnancy (14 weeks of amenorrhea).
- **How?** Your doctor or midwife gives you a three-part declaration form.
- **Where to send it?**
  * Two parts to your Health Insurance fund (CPAM)
  * One part to your Family Allowance Fund (CAF) or Agricultural Social Mutual Fund (MSA)

This declaration is essential because it triggers:
- 100% coverage of medical expenses related to pregnancy
- Opening of rights to maternity benefits
- Calculation of the maternity leave start date

### Mandatory Medical Follow-up

To benefit from full coverage, you must follow a specific medical path:

- **7 mandatory prenatal examinations**:
  * 1st exam before the end of the 3rd month
  * Then a monthly exam from the 4th month

- **3 recommended ultrasounds**:
  * 1st ultrasound: between 11 and 13 weeks of amenorrhea
  * 2nd ultrasound: between 22 and 24 weeks of amenorrhea
  * 3rd ultrasound: between 32 and 34 weeks of amenorrhea

- **Complementary exams**:
  * Trisomy 21 screening (optional)
  * Gestational diabetes screening
  * Regular blood and urine tests

All these exams are 100% covered by Health Insurance as part of the care pathway.

### Birth and Parenthood Preparation

- **8 birth preparation sessions** are 100% covered by Health Insurance
- **The early prenatal interview** (4th month) has been mandatory since 2020
- These sessions can be carried out by a midwife in private practice, at the hospital, or at PMI (Maternal and Child Protection)

## Work-Related Rights

### Maternity Leave

Maternity leave is a fundamental right for all working women:

#### Duration of Maternity Leave for Employees

- **First or second child**: 16 weeks (6 weeks before the expected delivery date, 10 weeks after)
- **Third child or more**: 26 weeks (8 weeks before, 18 weeks after)
- **Twins**: 34 weeks (12 weeks before, 22 weeks after)
- **Triplets or more**: 46 weeks (24 weeks before, 22 weeks after)

#### Compensation During Maternity Leave

- **For employees**: Daily allowances paid by Social Security, calculated based on the salary of the last 3 months.
- **For self-employed workers**: Lump sum maternal rest allowance + lump sum daily allowances.
- **For compensated job seekers**: Maintenance of unemployment benefits or payment of daily allowances depending on the situation.

#### Pathological Leave

In case of medical complications, additional leave can be prescribed:
- **Prenatal pathological leave**: Up to 2 weeks before the start of prenatal leave
- **Postnatal pathological leave**: Up to 4 weeks after the end of postnatal leave

### Paternity and Child Welcome Leave

Since 2021, paternity leave has been extended:
- **Total duration**: 25 calendar days (32 days for multiple births)
- **Mandatory period**: 4 consecutive days immediately after the 3-day birth leave
- **Optional period**: 21 days to be taken within 6 months following the birth

Compensation is similar to that of maternity leave, with a daily cap.

### Parental Education Leave

After birth, parents can benefit from parental leave:
- **Who can benefit?** Any employee with at least one year of seniority in the company
- **Duration**: Until the child is 3 years old
- **Possible forms**:
  * Total cessation of activity
  * Switch to part-time work
  * Possible splitting into periods of 1 to 6 months

This leave is not paid by the employer, but can be accompanied by the payment of PreParE (Shared Child Education Benefit) by the CAF.

## Financial Benefits

### During Pregnancy

#### Birth Grant (Prime à la naissance)

- **Amount**: €1,027.04 (2023 amount) for each unborn child
- **Conditions**: To be at least 5 months pregnant and have declared your pregnancy
- **Resources**: Subject to means testing
- **Payment**: Before the end of the 2nd month following birth

#### PAJE Base Allowance

- **Amount**:
  * Full rate: €184.87 per month (2023)
  * Partial rate: €92.44 per month (depending on resources)
- **Duration**: From the month of birth until the month preceding the 3rd birthday
- **Conditions**: Subject to means testing

### After Birth

#### Family Allowances

- **From 2 dependent children** under 20 years old
- **Amount varies according to**:
  * Number of children
  * Household resources
  * Age of children (increase from 14 years old)

#### Family Supplement

- **For large families** with at least 3 dependent children, all over 3 years old
- **Subject to means testing**

#### Back-to-School Allowance (ARS)

- **For school-going children** aged 6 to 18
- **Payment**: Late August, subject to conditions

### Childcare Aid

#### Supplement for Free Choice of Childcare (CMG)

- **For parents who**:
  * Employ a certified childminder (assistante maternelle)
  * Employ a home nanny
  * Use a micro-crèche

- **Amount**: Varies according to resources, child's age, and type of care
- **Coverage**: Between 15% and 85% of the cost

#### PreParE (Shared Child Education Benefit)

- **For parents who reduce or stop their activity** to care for their child
- **Conditions**: Having worked at least 8 quarters in the 2 to 4 years preceding birth
- **Amount**: Varies based on activity reduction (total stop vs part-time)

## Housing Aid

### Housing Allowances

Three types of aid exist (non-cumulative):
- **APL (Personalized Housing Aid)**
- **ALF (Family Housing Allowance)**
- **ALS (Social Housing Allowance)**

The arrival of a child modifies the calculation of these aids, generally upwards.

## Tax Benefits

### Family Quotient

- Each dependent child increases the number of tax shares of the household
- **First and second child**: 0.5 share each
- **Third child and following**: 1 share each

### Tax Credit for Childcare Costs

- **For children under 6** cared for outside the home
- **Amount**: 50% of expenses, up to a limit of €2,300 per child per year
- **Benefit**: Tax credit (refundable even if non-taxable)

### Tax Reduction for Home Employment

- **For employing a home nanny**
- **Amount**: 50% of expenses within an annual ceiling

## Specific Aid

### For Single-Parent Families

#### Family Support Allowance (ASF)

- **For children deprived of support** from one or both parents
- **Amount** (2023): €123.54 per month per child (single parent)
- **Not subject to means testing**

#### Increased RSA

- **For isolated parents** with dependent child(ren) or isolated pregnant women
- **Increase** in RSA amount for a limited period

### For Children with Disabilities

#### Education Allowance for Disabled Child (AEEH)

- **For children under 20** with a disability rate of at least 80%

## Steps After Birth

### Birth Declaration

- **Deadline**: 5 working days following delivery
- **Where**: At the town hall of the place of birth

### Civil Status and Social Security Registration

- **Social Security attachment**: Automatic after birth declaration
- **Carte Vitale**: Update necessary to include the child
- **Mutuelle (Health Insurance)**: Specific step to take with your complementary health insurance

### Leave and Benefits to Request

- **Paternity leave**: Request to be made to employer and Social Security at least 1 month before
- **Family benefits**: Update your CAF file with the child's birth certificate

## Conclusion

France has a particularly developed system of aid and rights to accompany expectant and new mothers. These schemes aim to financially secure this period of change and facilitate the balance between family and professional life.

Do not hesitate to inquire with competent bodies (CAF, CPAM, social services) to ensure you benefit from all the aid you are entitled to.
