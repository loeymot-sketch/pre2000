# Nutrition During Pregnancy: What to Eat and Avoid

Nutrition plays a crucial role during pregnancy, both for the health of the expectant mother and for the optimal development of the baby. A balanced and adapted diet helps meet the increased nutritional needs of this particular period, while avoiding certain risks for the fetus. This article guides you on foods to prioritize and those to avoid during these nine months.

## Nutritional Needs During Pregnancy

During pregnancy, energy and nutritional needs increase, but not excessively. Contrary to popular belief, the expectant mother should not "eat for two" in terms of quantity, but rather pay attention to the quality of her diet. Caloric needs only increase by about 300 calories per day starting from the second trimester, which is equivalent to a yogurt, an apple, and a slice of wholemeal bread.

Some nutrients become particularly important during pregnancy:

### Folic Acid (Vitamin B9)

Essential from the beginning of pregnancy and even before conception, folic acid helps prevent neural tube defects (such as spina bifida). It is found in green leafy vegetables (spinach, broccoli), legumes, citrus fruits, and fortified cereals. Supplementation is generally recommended before and during the first months of pregnancy.

### Iron

Iron needs double during pregnancy to support the increase in blood volume and prevent anemia. Food sources include red meats, legumes, dried fruits, and some green vegetables. Supplementation may be necessary if blood tests reveal a deficiency.

### Calcium

Essential for the formation of the baby's bones and teeth, calcium is found primarily in dairy products, but also in some green vegetables, almonds, sardines with bones, and calcium-rich mineral waters.

### Omega-3s

These essential fatty acids, particularly DHA, are important for the development of the fetus's brain and retina. Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardine) are rich in them, but be careful to choose species with low mercury content and limit their consumption.

### Proteins

Indispensable for the growth of maternal and fetal tissues, proteins must be consumed daily. They are found in meats, fish, eggs, dairy products, legumes, and whole grains.

### Iodine

Necessary for the proper functioning of the thyroid and the brain development of the fetus, iodine is found in seafood, dairy products, and iodized salt.

## Foods to Prioritize

To cover all these nutritional needs, here are the foods to prioritize during pregnancy:

### Fruits and Vegetables

Consume at least 5 portions per day, vary the colors to benefit from different nutrients. Prioritize seasonal products and if possible organic to limit exposure to pesticides.

### Whole Grains

Wholemeal bread, brown rice, wholemeal pasta, quinoa... These foods provide fiber, B vitamins, and essential minerals.

### Dairy Products

Milk, yogurts, pasteurized cheeses... Consume 3 to 4 portions per day to cover your calcium needs.

### Proteins

Alternate between lean meats, fish, eggs, and plant proteins (legumes, tofu). Aim for 2 portions per day.

### Quality Fats

Olive oil, rapeseed oil, nuts, avocado... Prioritize unsaturated fats beneficial for cardiovascular health and the baby's brain development.

### Water

Drink at least 1.5 to 2 liters of water per day to maintain good hydration and prevent constipation and urinary tract infections, which are more frequent during pregnancy.

## Foods to Avoid or Limit

Some foods present risks during pregnancy and should be avoided:

### Raw or Undercooked Foods of Animal Origin

To avoid toxoplasmosis (if you are not immune) and listeriosis, avoid:
- Raw or undercooked meats and fish (tartare, carpaccio, sushi)
- Raw seafood
- Raw or barely cooked eggs (homemade mayonnaise, traditional chocolate mousse)
- Soft cheeses made from raw milk (camembert, brie, roquefort)
- Raw cured meats (raw ham, dry sausage)

### Fish High in Mercury

Limit the consumption of swordfish, shark, marlin, and fresh tuna, which may contain high levels of mercury, potentially harmful to the neurological development of the fetus.

### Alcohol

No amount of alcohol is considered safe during pregnancy. Alcohol crosses the placenta and can cause malformations and developmental disorders in the fetus.

### Caffeine

Limit your consumption to 200 mg per day (about 2 cups of coffee). Caffeine is also found in tea, cola sodas, and chocolate.

### Foods Rich in Added Sugars and Saturated Fats

Limit pastries, baked goods, chips, sodas, and other ultra-processed foods, which provide many calories but few nutrients.

### Herbal Supplements and Certain Teas

Some plants can have harmful effects during pregnancy. Always consult a healthcare professional before taking herbal supplements or herbal teas.

## Managing Cravings and Food Aversions

Food cravings and aversions are common during pregnancy and are mainly due to hormonal changes. If you crave healthy foods, do not hesitate to satisfy them. If you desire less nutritious foods, try to consume them in moderation and balance them with healthier choices.

Food aversions can sometimes concern nutritionally important foods. In this case, look for alternatives that will provide you with the same nutrients. For example, if you can no longer stand meat, increase your consumption of legumes, eggs, or dairy products for proteins.

## Managing Weight Gain

Recommended weight gain during pregnancy depends on your body mass index (BMI) before pregnancy:
- BMI less than 18.5 (underweight): 12.5 to 18 kg
- BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 (normal weight): 11.5 to 16 kg
- BMI between 25 and 29.9 (overweight): 7 to 11.5 kg
- BMI greater than 30 (obesity): 5 to 9 kg

A balanced diet and regular physical activity (adapted to pregnancy) will help you maintain healthy weight gain.

## Conclusion

A balanced diet during pregnancy is essential for your health and the optimal development of your baby. Prioritize nutrient-rich foods, avoid those that present risks, and do not hesitate to consult a healthcare professional or a dietitian for personalized advice. Remember that the goal is not dietary perfection, but a global balance that will allow you to live this unique period of your life serenely.
