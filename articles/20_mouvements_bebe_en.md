# Baby's Movements During Pregnancy: What You Need to Know

Baby's movements in utero are one of the most fascinating and moving aspects of pregnancy. These little kicks, rolls, and jolts are much more than a simple source of wonder: they represent a precious indicator of your unborn child's health and well-being. This article explains when and how to perceive these movements, their evolution throughout pregnancy, and how to interpret them to ensure optimal monitoring of your baby.

## When Do You Start Feeling Baby Move?

### The First Movements

The first fetal movements, often called "quickening," are generally perceived between the 18th and 22nd weeks of amenorrhea (16-20 weeks of pregnancy). However, this timeframe can vary considerably from one woman to another:

- **For a first pregnancy**: Primiparas (women pregnant for the first time) usually feel the first movements later, often around 20-22 weeks, because they are not yet familiar with these sensations.

- **For subsequent pregnancies**: Multiparas (women who have already had children) often identify movements earlier, sometimes as early as 16-18 weeks, because they recognize these particular sensations.

### How Do the First Movements Manifest?

Initial sensations are often described as:
- "Fluttering" or "bubbles" in the belly
- Small "popcorn" popping
- A light touch, like the passage of a soap bubble
- An internal tickling

These subtle sensations can easily be confused with intestinal gurgling, which explains why they sometimes go unnoticed, especially during a first pregnancy.

### Factors Influencing Perception of Movements

Several elements can affect when you will start feeling your baby move:

- **Placenta location**: An anterior placenta (located on the front wall of the uterus) can "cushion" movements and delay their perception.
- **Maternal body shape**: Thin women may sometimes perceive movements earlier.
- **Activity level**: Very active women may notice movements later because they are less attentive to subtle sensations.
- **Baby's position**: Depending on the fetus's orientation, some movements may be more or less perceptible.

## Evolution of Movements Throughout Pregnancy

### Second Trimester (13-27 Weeks)

- **18-22 weeks**: First perceptible movements, light and irregular.
- **22-27 weeks**: Movements increasingly distinct and frequent.
- The baby still has plenty of space to move freely.

At this stage, there is no regular pattern, and you may not feel your baby for several hours, or even a whole day, without it being concerning.

### Early Third Trimester (28-34 Weeks)

- More vigorous and frequent movements.
- Perception of different types of movements: kicks, elbow nudges, rolls, hiccups.
- Progressive establishment of an activity pattern specific to your baby.
- Period of peak activity, generally between the 28th and 32nd week.

It is during this period that your baby is most active, with an average of 30 to 40 movements per hour during their waking periods.

### Late Pregnancy (35-40 Weeks)

- Change in the nature of movements: fewer large movements, more twists and stretches.
- Movements sometimes more uncomfortable or painful for the mother.
- Maintenance of movement frequency, even if their amplitude may decrease.

**Important**: Contrary to popular belief, movements do NOT decrease significantly at the end of pregnancy. Although space is more restricted, your baby continues to move regularly until delivery.

## Different Types of Fetal Movements

Throughout pregnancy, you will learn to distinguish different types of movements:

### Kicks
- Sharp and localized sensations.
- Often visible from the outside starting from the third trimester.
- Can be directed towards ribs, pelvis, or internal organs.

### Rolling
- Larger movements where the baby changes position.
- Wave-like sensation across the abdomen.
- Sometimes accompanied by a visible change in belly shape.

### Fetal Hiccups
- Small rhythmic and regular jerks.
- Repeat every 2-3 seconds.
- Generally last 5 to 15 minutes.
- Totally normal and harmless to the baby.

### Stretches
- Sensation of prolonged pressure.
- Impression that the baby is "pushing" against the uterine walls.
- Particularly frequent at the end of pregnancy.

## Importance of Monitoring Movements

### Why Monitor Baby's Movements?

Fetal movements constitute an excellent indicator of your baby's well-being:
- They testify to good functioning of the central nervous system.
- They indicate that the baby is receiving enough oxygen and nutrients.
- A significant change can signal a potential problem.

Studies have shown that a notable decrease in fetal movements can precede fetal distress in some cases. This is why health professionals recommend being attentive to your baby's movements, particularly in the third trimester.

### When to Start Monitoring Movements?

Active monitoring of movements is generally recommended from 28 weeks, when movements are well established and the baby has developed a relatively predictable activity pattern.

## Movement Counting Methods

Several methods exist to monitor your baby's movements. Here are the most common:

### The "Count-to-Ten" Method

- Choose a time of day when your baby is usually active.
- Settle comfortably, preferably semi-reclined on your left side.
- Note the start time and count up to 10 distinct movements.
- Note the time you reached 10 movements.
- Ideally, you should perceive 10 movements in less than 2 hours.

### The "Two Hours" Method

- Choose a period of 2 consecutive hours each day.
- Count all movements felt during this period.
- You should normally perceive at least 10 movements.

### The Qualitative Approach

Rather than strict counting, some professionals simply recommend being attentive to your baby's usual activity pattern and reporting any significant change.

## When to Worry and Consult?

### Warning Signs

Consult a health professional quickly if you notice:
- A total absence of movements for more than 12 hours.
- A significant decrease in movements compared to your baby's usual pattern.
- Fewer than 10 movements felt over a 2-hour period (after 28 weeks).
- A sudden change in the type or intensity of movements.

### What to Do in Case of Concern?

1. **Take a moment of calm**: Lie down on your left side in a quiet environment.
2. **Focus** on movements for 1 to 2 hours.
3. **Drink a sweet drink** or have a snack (can stimulate baby's activity).
4. **If you still do not perceive satisfactory movements**, contact your midwife, doctor, or go to the maternity ward immediately.

Never put it off until tomorrow: in case of doubt, it is always better to consult, even if it turns out to be a false alarm.

## Factors Influencing Baby's Movements

### Sleep and Wake Cycles

- Fetuses have sleep cycles of 20 to 40 minutes.
- Alternating with periods of active wakefulness.
- Towards the end of pregnancy, these cycles become more regular and predictable.

### Influence of Maternal Diet

- A snack or meal can stimulate fetal activity.
- Glucose often causes an increase in movements.
- Certain cold or sweet drinks can trigger reactions.

### Influence of Maternal Activities

- **Position**: Lying on the left side, you will generally perceive more movements.
- **Physical activity**: While you are active, you may be less attentive to movements.
- **Stress and emotions**: Can influence baby's activity.
- **Loud sounds**: Can cause startle reactions in the fetus.

## Conclusion

Your baby's movements represent much more than a simple manifestation of their presence: they are a privileged means of communication between you and them, and a precious indicator of their well-being. Learning to recognize, appreciate, and monitor them is an integral part of the pregnancy experience.

Every baby has their own activity pattern, and you will quickly become an expert at recognizing what is normal for yours. This intimate knowledge is a precious tool to ensure their well-being until birth.

Never hesitate to consult in case of doubt about your baby's movements. Health professionals always prefer to verify that everything is fine rather than take the slightest risk. Your maternal intuition is a precious guide - trust it.
